Benefits of Teaching in the UK as a Foreigner

When you teach in the UK, you are entitled to;

  1. Apply for £10,000 International Relocation Payment (IRP)
  2. Resident permit to live and work in the UK
  3. easy access to Skilled Worker Visas
  4. Teacher Training Funding
  5. Build your career and connect with teams of experts.

What Qualifies You to Become a Teacher in the UK?

To become a teacher in the UK, the following documents are required;

  • If you want to teach Primary in the UK, have GCSEs at grade 4 (C) or above in English, and Maths and science.
  • You can also possess a degree to teach primary and secondary; if you have one or an equivalent qualification, you can do postgraduate teacher training.
  • Intended teachers without a degree can do undergraduate teacher training to get a degree along with qualified teacher status (QTS).

What Certificate Do I Need to Teach in the UK?

To teach in the UK, you need the following certificates;

  1. Qualified Teacher Status (QTS) to teach in maintained primary, secondary, and special schools in England.
  2. QTS with Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE)
  3. Postgraduate Certificate in Education (PGCE) without Qualified Teacher Status (QTS).
  4. Have language skills
  5. permitted to work in United Kingdom
  6. Have a skilled worker visa

How to Apply for Teaching Jobs in UK

To apply for teaching jobs in the UK, several options are available, including jobs in Primary, Maths, Science, Teaching Assistant, and Headteacher. In all, to start application, you must;

  • Visit the UK teaching jobs Vacancies website
  • Select your preferred Teaching job
  • Apply through the School of Choice
  • Attend interview
  • Submit all necessary documents as required
  • Apply for Visa
  • Start Teaching
