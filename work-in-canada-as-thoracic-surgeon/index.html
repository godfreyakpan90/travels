Who is a Thoracic Surgeon?

A specialized thoracic surgeon is responsible for addressing cases of lung and esophageal cancer, whereas surgeons with expertise in the field focus on treating heart-related conditions. This is easy, right?

Duties of A Thoracic Surgeon

The main job role of a thoracic surgeon in Canada is to diagnose and treat the following diseases or injuries:

  • Pericardium (the membrane around the heart)
  • Trachea (windpipe)
  • Esophagus (the tube leading to the stomach)
  • Lungs
  • Pleura (membranes around the lungs)
  • Mediastinum (the area that separates the lungs and holds the heart)
  • Diaphragm
  • Chest wall
  • Coronary arteries, valves, and myocardium

What Do you Need to Work in Canada as a Thoracic Surgeon?

To work as a Thoracic Surgeon in Canada, you need:

  • Medical degree
  • Medical license
  • 5-year General Surgery residency
  • 1-2 year Thoracic Surgery fellowship
  • Certification from RCPSC
  • Clinical experience
  • Language proficiency
  • Professionalism
  • Continuing education
